Privacy Policy

Last updated: 30 March 2026

The short version: Your accounting data lives entirely in your own Google Drive. We never see it, store it, or share it. The only personal data we hold is your email address, used solely to identify you within the app.

1. Who we are

no~bull books is operated by no~bull consulting, a sole trader business operated by Edward Jenkins (edward@nobull.consulting).

For the purposes of UK GDPR and EU GDPR, no~bull consulting is the data controller.

2. What data we collect and why

2.1 Your Google account email address

When you sign up, your Google account email is stored in your workspace spreadsheet to identify you as the account owner. This is used solely to:

  • Grant you access to your workspace
  • Assign you the Owner role within the application
  • Manage your account status (trial, active, suspended)

2.2 Your accounting data

All invoices, bills, client records, bank transactions and other accounting data you enter into no~bull books is stored exclusively in a Google Sheets spreadsheet in your own Google Drive. We do not have access to this data except as required to operate the application on your behalf (for example, to save a new invoice you create).

2.3 Usage logs

Google Apps Script automatically logs application execution data (function names, durations, errors) for debugging purposes. These logs are retained for 7 days and are not shared with third parties.

3. Legal basis for processing (UK & EU GDPR)

  • Contract performance — processing your email and operating the application is necessary to provide the service you have signed up for
  • Legitimate interests — maintaining execution logs to diagnose errors and keep the service running reliably

4. How your data is stored

Your accounting data is stored in Google Drive under your own Google account. no~bull books operates on Google's infrastructure (Google Apps Script and Google Sheets). Google's privacy policy applies to the infrastructure layer: policies.google.com/privacy.

Your email address is stored in the Settings sheet of your workspace spreadsheet, which sits in your own Google Drive.

5. Data sharing

We do not sell, rent or share your personal data with any third parties, except:

  • Google — as the infrastructure provider (Google Apps Script, Google Drive, Google Sheets)
  • HMRC — if you use the Making Tax Digital (MTD) features, VAT and income tax data is submitted directly to HMRC via their API. This submission is initiated by you and only contains data you have explicitly selected for submission.

6. Data retention

Your accounting data remains in your Google Drive for as long as you choose to keep it. You have full control — you can delete the spreadsheet at any time.

If you close your no~bull books account, your email address will be removed from our registry within 30 days of your request.

7. Your rights (UK & EU GDPR)

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — ask us to delete your personal data
  • Right to restriction — ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to portability — your accounting data is already in your own Google Drive in a standard format (Google Sheets / CSV)
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at edward@nobull.consulting. We will respond within 30 days.

If you are in the EU, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. If you are in the UK, you may complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

8. Cookies

no~bull books does not use cookies or any client-side tracking technologies. The application runs entirely within Google's infrastructure and does not set cookies on your device.

9. Children's privacy

no~bull books is intended for use by adults operating businesses. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under the age of 18.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will notify active users of material changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the service after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
